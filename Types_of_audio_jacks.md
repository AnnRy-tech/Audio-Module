Audio jacks are small connectors that allow sound to travel from one device to another. They connect headphones, speakers, microphones, and musical equipment to phones, laptops, amplifiers, and more.
At their core, they work by transferring audio signals through metal contact points inside the plug and the port.


Even with wireless audio growing fast, wired connections are still trusted for:



Stable sound quality

Zero charging worries

Low delay in gaming and music production


Most creators and musicians still rely on audio jacks due to reliability and clarity.

TS, TRS and TRRS Explained Simply
You might notice black rings on the metal part of a plug. These indicate how many contact points it has.

Here’s how they work:

TS: Mono sound, used for simple microphones and guitars
TRS: Stereo sound, common in standard headphones
TRRS: Stereo sound plus microphone, used in headsets
Understanding this helps you identify different types of jacks just by looking at them.
